Card Backs — Why & When to Adjust
MR1 · Back
Landscapes
Why f/8?
Lens sweet spot. Max sharpness + deep DoF. Avoid f/16+ (diffraction blur).
Why ISO 100?
Base ISO = cleanest image. Camera controls shutter speed automatically.
Why AF-S?
Landscapes don't move. Lock focus once, compose, shoot.
Why SteadyShot OFF on tripod?
IS on a tripod causes micro-vibrations that blur the image.
When to adjust in the field
Clouds moving fast → raise shutter speed manually.
Very dark scene → allow ISO up to 400.
Want creative blur (waterfall) → use shutter priority instead.
MR2 · Back
Wildlife
Why Manual + Auto ISO?
You guarantee aperture (DoF) AND shutter (freeze motion). ISO floats to compensate.
Why f/6.3?
Maximum aperture at 350mm. No choice — gather all available light.
Why 1/1000s?
Freezes walking/perched animals. Noisy sharp photo beats clean blurry one.
Why silent mode?
Avoids startling animals. Electronic shutter = zero noise.
When to adjust in the field
Bird in flight → shutter to 1/2000–1/4000.
Perched in low light → shutter to 1/500.
Panning shot → lower to 1/250 for motion blur background.
MR3 · Back
General / Street
Why Aperture Priority?
Walk-around doesn't need fast shutter. You pick the DoF, camera handles the rest.
Why f/5.6?
Sharp + versatile. Not so narrow it loses light, not so wide backgrounds distract.
Why min SS 1/125?
Keeps moving people sharp. Camera won't go below this — ISO compensates.
Why Wide focus area?
Lets the camera find subjects anywhere in the frame. Human recognition guides it.
When to adjust in the field
Dark interior → open to f/2.8.
Portrait of someone → switch to M1 instead.
Busy scene, no people → turn Subject Recog off.
M1 · Back
Portrait / People
Why f/2.8?
Widest aperture on 18-50mm = maximum subject/background separation (bokeh).
Why Eye AF?
Eyes are the most critical part of a portrait. Camera locks on them automatically.
Why min SS 1/125?
Prevents subject motion blur even when people shift slightly.
Future upgrade
Sigma 56mm f/1.4 (~$400) or Sony 50mm f/1.8 (~$250) for stronger bokeh.
When to adjust in the field
Group photo → stop down to f/5.6 so everyone is sharp.
Very dark → let ISO go higher, keep f/2.8.
Child running → switch to AF-C.
M2 · Back
Low Light / Dusk-Dawn
Why wide open?
In low light, gathering light is priority #1. DoF is secondary.
Why min SS 1/60?
Handheld limit for wide lenses. Below 1/60 = camera shake blur.
Why 11mm first?
f/1.8 gathers 2.25× more light than f/2.8 lenses. Makes a huge difference.
Why Long Exp NR off?
At 1/60s+ we never trigger long-exposure NR. Leaving it off avoids future delays.
When to adjust in the field
Static scene, camera on surface → drop to 1/15.
Moving people → keep 1/60 or raise it.
Almost dark → switch to M3 (Night Sky) if on tripod.
M3 · Back
Night Sky / Stars ★
Why f/1.8?
Gathers 2.25× more light than f/2.8. Stars need every photon.
Why 20 seconds?
500 rule: 500÷11mm = 45s max. 20s is safe — stars stay round, not trails.
Why ISO 3200?
Starting point. Dark sky → try 1600. Light pollution → try 6400.
Why Daylight WB?
AWB makes stars orange. Daylight = neutral white stars, natural sky color.
Why 2s timer?
Pressing shutter causes vibration. 2s delay lets the camera settle.
When to adjust in the field
Moon up → shorten to 10s.
Very dark sky → try 25s.
Stars trailing → shorten shutter.
M4 · Back
Video — Wildlife
Why 1/125s shutter?
180° rule: shutter = 2× frame rate. 60p → 1/125s = natural motion blur.
Why 60p?
Smooth motion + slow-motion option in post (play at 30p = 2× slow motion).
Why Active SteadyShot?
Maximum stabilization for handheld video with a long telephoto lens.
S-Log3 option
If color grading in post: switch Picture Profile to S-Log3. Image will look flat/grey on screen — that's normal.
When to adjust in the field
Want cinematic look → switch to 30p / 1/60s.
Color grading in post → switch to S-Log3.
Noisy audio → check wind noise reduction.
